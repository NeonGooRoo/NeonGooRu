Information about Japanese

Dictionaries and translators

Yomitan search - Search inside your Yomitan dictionaries. Can be opened by pressing the extension icon and clicking on magnifying glass.

DeepLopen in new window and Google Translateopen in new window are 2 best machine translation tools, and both have pros and cons.

Immersion Kitopen in new window - Search for a word in anime clips with translations. You can copy cards into Anki.

Filmotopen in new window - Search for words in YouTube videos. Words of 2+ Kanji should be to put into quotation marks, and you should choose the Japanese language. A very big advantage - you can choose to parse only manually created subtitles.

Youglishopen in new window - The same as Filmot, but autogenerated and handmade subs are mixed together.

JPDB.ioopen in new window - a very convenient primarily English Dictionary (has some other languages and even a monolingual dictionary but with less entries), a convenient search for Kanji and readings, you can use instead of Anki for flashcards, see guide here.

Jishoopen in new window - A very good web bilingual dictionary.

Gooopen in new window - Very big bilingual AND monolingual dictionary.

Kotobankopen in new window - Japanese Encyclopedia-Dictionary.

Glosbeopen in new window - Dictionary with translations of sentences made by the community.

weblioopen in new window - A huge bilingual dictionary with examples.

Reversoopen in new window - Examples of the word in sentences and translations of sentences.

AI chat bots are also able to translate and explain some things, but imperfect.

Grammar

IMABIopen in new window - A modern alternative for Tae Kim's guide, it is probably a better option.

Tae kim's Guideopen in new window - The most popular guide, was the best option for beginners, but very short. There are some inaccuracies and strange interpretations, but you will see people talk about it everywhere.

Tae Kim's Guide open in new window (Anki) - Most of the guide above, but in the form of flashcards. I myself started with it, it is very convenient. Also, some inaccuracies of the original guide are fixed there.

Mainichi Nonbiriopen in new window - The website is completely on Japanese, a lot of information and grammar sorted by JLPT levels

Cure Dolly‚Äôs Organic Japaneseopen in new window - A very good guide showing Japanese grammar from a completely different perspective. Personally, a huge number of things there are explained in a way that just makes sense. The only downside is the quality of the audio.

Cure Dolly Complete Transcriptopen in new window - Transcript of the videos above, if it is easier to read or you dislike her voice.

Organic Japanese with Cure Dollyopen in new window - Cure Dolly's website, lots of good info.

BunProopen in new window - A very convenient tool for learning Grammar, has SRS for a subscription (SRS is not recommended)

Maggie Senseiopen in new window - A website with a bunch of grammar info.

Tofuguopen in new window - The site with info suited more for beginners, many different interesting posts.

Listening

Condensed Audio

This is an audio taken from anime or other video content, pauses between the lines of dialogue are cut out of it. For beginners, it is a great option, it is much easier than podcasts. It is recommended to listen to stuff you've already watched.
